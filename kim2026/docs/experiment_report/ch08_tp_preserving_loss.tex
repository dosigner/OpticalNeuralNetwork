\chapter{Throughput-Preserving Loss Design}

\section{From 0401 Failure to a New Principle}

The \texttt{0401} rerun showed that detector utility collapses when throughput is not
protected. The \texttt{0402-focal-new-losses-pitchrescale-3strat-cn2-5e14} campaign
turned that observation into a design principle by comparing three losses:
absolute bucket power, PIB with explicit throughput preservation, and a
multi-plane hybrid objective.

\section{The Design Principle}

\begin{breakthrough}
Throughput must be intrinsic to the loss, not a post-hoc diagnostic.
\end{breakthrough}

\texttt{focal\_absolute\_bucket} and \texttt{focal\_tp\_penalized\_pib} both keep throughput near
98\% and raise the 10\,$\mu$m received power by roughly 5.8--5.9\% above the
turbulent baseline. By contrast, the multi-plane objective appears attractive in
PIB and Strehl, but it collapses throughput and therefore fails the detector.
