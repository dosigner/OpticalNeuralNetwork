\section{Introduction and Motivation}
% ============================================================

A static diffractive neural network (D2NN) consisting of $L=5$ phase-only layers aims to improve the coupling efficiency of a turbulence-degraded beam into a single-mode fiber (SMF) at the focal plane of a receiving telescope. The central question is:

\begin{quote}
\textit{Can a static, passive phase-only D2NN increase the absolute received power within a 10\,$\mu$m fiber bucket at the focal plane, compared to no correction at all?}
\end{quote}

This report documents three sweep campaigns (0401, 0402, 0403) encompassing 12 distinct loss functions that reveal a critical physics insight: \textbf{the standard PIB metric is misleading because it ignores throughput}, and only loss functions that explicitly preserve energy can achieve genuine received power improvement.

\subsection{Key Optical Relations}

The Fried parameter at the receiver:
\begin{equation}
r_0 = \left(0.423\, k^2\, C_n^2\, L_{\mathrm{prop}}\right)^{-3/5} \approx 3.0\;\mathrm{cm}, \quad D_{\mathrm{tel}}/r_0 \approx 5.0
\end{equation}

The Airy disk radius at the focal plane:
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{Airy}} = 1.22 \frac{\lambda f}{D} = 4.25\;\mu\mathrm{m}
\end{equation}

Theoretical encircled energy within $r_b = 10\;\mu$m for vacuum (Airy pattern):
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{EE}(r_b) = 1 - J_0^2\!\left(\frac{\pi D r_b}{\lambda f}\right) - J_1^2\!\left(\frac{\pi D r_b}{\lambda f}\right) = 93.2\%
\end{equation}

The turbulent seeing spot at the focal plane:
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{seeing}} = \frac{\lambda f}{r_{0,\mathrm{eff}}} \approx 17.5\;\mu\mathrm{m}
\end{equation}
which exceeds the 10\,$\mu$m bucket, causing energy spillover and reducing the turbulent PIB to $\sim$76\%.

\section{The Fundamental Problem: PIB vs.\ Received Power}
\label{sec:problem}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Metric Definitions}

\textbf{Power-in-Bucket (PIB)} is a \textit{ratio}:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{PIB}(r_b) = \frac{\int_0^{r_b} I_f(r)\, r\, dr}{\int_0^{\infty} I_f(r)\, r\, dr}
\end{equation}
where $I_f(r)$ is the focal-plane irradiance. PIB can increase even if total energy decreases, as long as the remaining energy is well concentrated.

\textbf{Received Power (RP)} is the \textit{absolute} energy in the bucket:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{RP}(r_b) = \sum_{\mathbf{r} \in \mathcal{B}(r_b)} |U_f(\mathbf{r})|^2
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{B}(r_b)$ is the set of focal-plane pixels within radius $r_b$.

\textbf{Throughput (TP)} is the energy preservation ratio:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{TP} = \frac{\sum |U_{\mathrm{out}}|^2}{\sum |U_{\mathrm{in}}|^2}
\end{equation}

The critical relationship:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\mathrm{RP}(r_b) \approx \mathrm{PIB}(r_b) \times \mathrm{TP} \times E_{\mathrm{in}}}
\label{eq:rp}
\end{equation}

A loss function that maximizes PIB alone can achieve this by \textit{scattering} aberrated energy outside the aperture, reducing TP. The net RP may decrease even as PIB increases.

\subsection{Phase-Only Mask Energy Scattering Mechanism}

A single phase-only layer applies $U_{\mathrm{out}}(\mathbf{r}) = e^{j\phi(\mathbf{r})}\, U_{\mathrm{in}}(\mathbf{r})$, which satisfies $|U_{\mathrm{out}}| = |U_{\mathrm{in}}|$ pointwise. However, after free-space propagation between layers, the angular spectrum redistributes energy. Phase gratings can deflect light outside the finite computational window, effectively acting as an amplitude filter. With 5 cascaded layers and inter-layer propagation, the D2NN has sufficient degrees of freedom to scatter $>$98\% of the input energy while preserving a clean residual beam.

\section{Sweep 0401: Conventional Loss Functions}
\label{sec:0401}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Loss Function Definitions}

\noindent\textbf{A. Focal PIB Only:}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}} = 1 - \mathrm{PIB}(r_b)
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{B. Focal Strehl Only:}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{Strehl}} = 1 - S, \quad S = \frac{I_f(\mathbf{0})}{I_{f,\mathrm{DL}}(\mathbf{0})}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{C. Focal Intensity Overlap:}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IO}} = 1 - \frac{\left(\sum I_{\mathrm{pred}} \cdot I_{\mathrm{target}}\right)^2}{\sum I_{\mathrm{pred}}^2 \cdot \sum I_{\mathrm{target}}^2}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{D. CO + PIB Hybrid:}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{hybrid}} = \underbrace{(1 - \mathrm{CO})}_{\text{output plane}} + \; 0.5 \cdot \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}}}_{\text{focal plane}}
\end{equation}
where Complex Overlap at the D2NN output plane:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{CO} = \frac{|\langle U_{\mathrm{pred}}, U_{\mathrm{vac}} \rangle|^2}{\|U_{\mathrm{pred}}\|^2 \, \|U_{\mathrm{vac}}\|^2}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Physics Interpretation}

The D2NN exploits inter-layer diffraction to act as a spatial filter. Consider the angular spectrum decomposition after layer $\ell$:
\begin{equation}
\tilde{U}_\ell(f_x, f_y) = \mathcal{F}\{e^{j\phi_\ell} \cdot U_\ell\}
\end{equation}
The free-space transfer function $H(f_x, f_y; d) = e^{jk_z d}$ preserves evanescent components within the passband $\sqrt{f_x^2 + f_y^2} < 1/\lambda$. However, phase gratings with spatial frequency $f_g > 1/(2\Delta x)$ can alias energy outside the computational window, which is numerically equivalent to absorption.

By cascading 5 such layers, the D2NN creates an effective amplitude-phase filter despite each individual layer being phase-only. When optimizing PIB alone, the gradient naturally discovers that \textit{removing} aberrated light (lowering the denominator in PIB) is easier than \textit{correcting} it.

\section{Sweep 0402: Throughput-Aware Loss Functions}
\label{sec:0402}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Rationale for New Loss Designs}

The 0401 failure motivated three new approaches, all designed to prevent the energy-scattering collapse:

\noindent\textbf{E. Absolute Bucket Loss} --- ratio to vacuum bucket energy:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{abs}} = 1 - \frac{\sum_{\mathbf{r} \in \mathcal{B}} |U_{f,\mathrm{pred}}(\mathbf{r})|^2}{\sum_{\mathbf{r} \in \mathcal{B}} |U_{f,\mathrm{vac}}(\mathbf{r})|^2}
\label{eq:abs_bucket}
\end{equation}

\textit{Physics rationale:} Unlike normalized PIB, the numerator is absolute energy. Scattering energy away reduces the numerator directly, so the loss cannot be minimized by filtering --- it must concentrate \textit{existing} energy into the bucket.

\noindent\textbf{F. TP-Penalized PIB} ($w = 1.0$):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{TP\text{-}PIB}} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}} + w \cdot \max(1 - \mathrm{TP},\; 0)
\end{equation}

\textit{Physics rationale:} Explicit throughput constraint as a Lagrangian penalty. The $\max(\cdot, 0)$ clamp allows TP $>$ 1 (possible due to numerical artifacts) without penalty.

\noindent\textbf{G. Multiplane CO + PIB + Amplitude:}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{multi}} = 0.5\,(1 - \mathrm{CO}) + 0.3\,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}} + 0.2\,\|A_{\mathrm{pred}} - A_{\mathrm{vac}}\|^2
\end{equation}

\textit{Physics rationale:} The amplitude MSE term $\|A_{\mathrm{pred}} - A_{\mathrm{vac}}\|^2$ implicitly preserves throughput by penalizing amplitude deviations from the vacuum envelope.

\section{Sweep 0403: Loss Function Optimization}
\label{sec:0403}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Expanded Loss Function Space}

Building on the 0402 success, we tested 5 additional formulations:

\noindent\textbf{H. Absolute Bucket + CO} (equal weight):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{abs+CO}} = 0.5\,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{abs}} + 0.5\,(1 - \mathrm{CO})
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{I. PIB + Hard TP} (quadratic penalty, $\lambda = 10$):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{hardTP}} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}} + 10 \cdot \left[\max(1 - \mathrm{TP},\;0)\right]^2
\end{equation}

\textit{Rationale:} Quadratic penalty creates a steeper gradient near TP $< 1$, preventing gradual throughput erosion during training.

\noindent\textbf{J. TP-Penalized PIB} with weight sweep ($w \in \{0.5, 2.0\}$):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{w} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PIB}} + w \cdot \max(1 - \mathrm{TP},\; 0)
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{K. Raw Received Power} (no vacuum reference):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{rawRP}} = -\log\!\left(\frac{\sum_{\mathbf{r} \in \mathcal{B}} |U_{f,\mathrm{pred}}(\mathbf{r})|^2}{\sum |U_{\mathrm{in}}|^2} + \epsilon\right)
\label{eq:raw_rp}
\end{equation}

\textit{Physics rationale:} This is the \textit{most direct} formulation --- it maximizes the focal-plane throughput into the fiber bucket without any reference to the vacuum field. The logarithm provides scale-invariant gradients.

\subsection{Per-Loss-Function Insights}

\subsubsection{Raw Received Power (Best: $+6.4\%$)}

The raw RP loss (Eq.~\ref{eq:raw_rp}) achieves the highest received power because it directly optimizes the quantity of interest without intermediate proxies. Key properties:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item No vacuum reference $\Rightarrow$ no ratio artifacts when vacuum is much higher than achievable
\item Log-scale gradient: $\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathrm{RP}} \propto -1/\mathrm{RP}$, providing stronger gradients when RP is low
\item Inherently throughput-preserving: scattering energy \textit{reduces} the numerator
\end{itemize}

The weakest TP constraint allows the D2NN to trade more throughput for PIB:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]

\item Net RP is lowest because the TP reduction outweighs the PIB gain
\end{itemize}

This confirms the relationship in Eq.~\ref{eq:rp}: \textbf{TP preservation is more impactful than PIB maximization} for received power.

\begin{itemize}[nosep]

\item The CO term competes with the bucket term, slightly reducing RP
\end{itemize}

\textit{Conclusion:} At TP $\approx 98\%$, adding CO provides negligible benefit and slightly harms received power.

\section{Physical Mechanism: Focal Energy Redistribution}
\label{sec:mechanism}
% ============================================================

The $+6\%$ received power improvement arises from a subtle mechanism. Consider the turbulent focal field as a superposition of a coherent core and a speckle halo:
\begin{equation}
U_f = U_{\mathrm{core}} + U_{\mathrm{halo}}
\end{equation}

The D2NN modifies the pupil-plane field such that after the focusing lens:
\begin{equation}
U_f' = U_{\mathrm{core}}' + U_{\mathrm{halo}}'
\end{equation}
where $|U_{\mathrm{core}}'|^2 > |U_{\mathrm{core}}|^2$ within $r_b$, at the expense of $|U_{\mathrm{halo}}'|^2$ being redistributed to larger radii (but still within the focal-plane window, not lost).

Since $\mathrm{TP} \approx 0.99$, the total energy is conserved. The D2NN effectively applies a weak spatial filter that tightens the PSF core by a few percent, which is sufficient to push more energy inside the 10\,$\mu$m bucket.

This is not wavefront correction (which would require matching the turbulent phase), but rather a \textbf{statistical mode-filtering} operation: the static phase masks are optimized over the ensemble of turbulent realizations to find a configuration that, on average, redirects a small fraction of the halo energy back into the core.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
% ============================================================

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{PIB is misleading.} Any loss function that only maximizes the power-in-bucket \textit{ratio} will exploit the energy-scattering loophole, achieving high PIB at the cost of catastrophic throughput loss and net received power degradation.

\item \textbf{Throughput preservation is essential.} Only loss functions that explicitly constrain or inherently preserve throughput can achieve genuine received power improvement. The critical threshold is $\mathrm{TP} > 95\%$.

\item \textbf{Static D2NN can improve received power by $\sim$6\%.} For 1\,km propagation with $C_n^2 = 5 \times 10^{-14}$, a 5-layer phase-only D2NN increases the absolute focal-plane received power within a 10\,$\mu$m bucket by 5.5--6.4\% over the uncorrected turbulent beam.

\item \textbf{The mechanism is energy redistribution, not wavefront correction.} WF RMS and Zernike decomposition are unchanged. The D2NN applies a weak, ensemble-optimized spatial filter that tightens the PSF core by a few percent.

\item \textbf{Raw received power loss is optimal.} The direct formulation $\mathcal{L} = -\log(E_b / E_{\mathrm{in}})$ achieves the best performance ($+6.4\%$) with high throughput (98.6\%) and low variance, without requiring vacuum reference fields.

\item \textbf{TP penalty weight has diminishing returns.} Beyond $w \geq 2.0$ (or $\lambda \geq 10$ quadratic), all strategies converge to the same $\sim$6\% attractor, suggesting a fundamental physical limit of static phase-only D2NN for this turbulence regime.
\end{enumerate}
